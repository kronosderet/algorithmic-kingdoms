## Log Analysis Findings

1. **Controls not discoverable:** Game 3 is the smoking gun — 5 minutes of pure wood gathering, zero interactions with any other system. The player didn't know how to build or train.

2. **Worker flee/resume spam:** 40+ events per incident in Game 2. Workers oscillate between flee and resume states when enemies are near gather points. Creates visual chaos and economic disruption.

3. **Enemy rank-ups feel unfair:** Enemies rank up to Corporal by attacking unchallenged Town Hall. New players have no military to contest, so enemies get free rank-ups making subsequent waves harder.

4. **Idle military doesn't auto-intercept:** Soldiers and archers don't automatically engage enemies attacking the Town Hall unless enemies enter their (short) aggro range. Trained military sits idle while TH burns.

5. **Formation discovery lost:** Game 2 achieved the first-ever external formation discovery, but the notification flashed and disappeared. The tester didn't understand what happened or how to use it.

6. **No towers in any game:** The defensive tower system was never used. Either not discoverable or not intuitive to build.

7. **Starting gold unspent:** 300 gold at death in Game 3 means the player had resources but no knowledge of what to spend them on.

---

## Tester Feedback (Verbal)

1. **Map too big for easy mode** — Scale map size with difficulty (64² easy, 128² medium, 256² hard)
2. **No tutorial/hints** — Need first-run overlay explaining basic controls
3. **No command feedback** — Issuing commands (move, gather, build, train) gives no visual/audio confirmation
4. **Formation discovery not memorable** — Needs to be a major event (camera pause, persistent UI, preview animation)
5. **No message history** — Notifications flash and vanish. Need scrollable color-coded log
6. **Edge scrolling frustrating** — Too thin margin, bottom panel and minimap trigger unwanted panning *(FIXED)*
7. **Focus on UX and user journey** — Deep systems but hostile surface
8. **Likes:** Fractals, originality, Tree of Life concept — visual identity lands
9. **Unit colors confusing** — Wardens too similar to enemies. Rainbow scheme requested *(FIXED)*

---

## Actions Taken

- [x] #6 — Edge scroll margin increased to 25px, minimap/panel exclusion zones added (camera.py)
- [x] #9 — Rainbow unit colors: Violet gatherer, Indigo warden, Blue ranger. Enemy dark mirrors. (constants.py, unit.py)
- [x] #1 — Map scale by difficulty (64x64 easy, 128x128 medium, 256x256 hard)
- [x] #2 — Progressive tutorial: 7 hints at natural breakpoints (TH, gather, build, train, defend)
- [x] #3 — Command confirmation effects: expanding rings (move/attack/gather/build/rally)
- [x] #4 — Formation discovery as major event: slowmo + center banner + persistent log
- [x] #5 — Message log: 3-line mini in bottom-right panel, click for full overlay with timestamps
- [ ] #7 — Full UX pass

---

## Significance

This is the **first playtest by a human other than the developer**. The results confirm that the game's deep mathematical systems (formations, harmony, resonance) are invisible to new players. The surface UX is the bottleneck, not the depth. The tester's positive reaction to the visual identity (fractals, algorithmic art, Tree of Life) validates the aesthetic direction.
